I am broadly interested in using geospatial data and machine learning for a wide range of applications.
Some of the key topics I have worked on or continue to explore include:

## Floods
I have built a tool called Global Flood Mapper that maps flood extent using Sentinel-1 SAR imagery as soon
as it becomes available, and enables impact assessment using land cover and gridded population datasets.
Users can download the resulting maps in geospatial formats or share an interactive map with others for
exact reproducibility, all in the cloud without having to download anything or write a single line of code.

Beyond mapping flood extent, I am also deeply interested in assessing the impacts of floods. My doctoral
dissertation examines the causal impacts of floods on agricultural productivity.

## Agriculture
As part of my doctoral work, I am trying to generate high-resolution agriculture maps in smallholder,
data-scarce settings. Lately, I have been exploring the use of foundation models under zero-shot and
few-shot learning settings to generate reliable agriculture maps.

## Urban Analysis
Earlier in my research career, I worked extensively on urban spatial analysis, including estimating
city-scale building heights from satellite imagery, modeling urban growth and land-use change, extracting
road network hierarchy and connectivity, and studying drivers of urban air pollution. This work shaped how
I approach spatial problems more broadly, and while my interests have since grown to span a wider range of
human and environmental challenges, urban systems remain a lens I still bring to new research questions.

## Open-Source Tools
I am passionate about open-source geospatial software development and have created tools to support
research and real-world applications. Some of my notable projects include:

- [PyRSGIS](https://github.com/PratyushTripathy/pyrsgis) – A Python package for raster data processing, soon to include ML workflows for geospatial analysis
- [Global Flood Mapper](https://github.com/PratyushTripathy/global_flood_mapper) – A Google Earth Engine tool for rapid flood mapping
- [COINS](https://github.com/PratyushTripathy/COINS) – A Python-based tool for street network continuity analysis

I am always looking to improve and expand these tools. If you're interested in collaboration or have ideas,
feel free to reach out!
